## Persona 1: "Seeker Sarah" — The Anonymous Help-Seeker

### Demographics
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | 22 |
| **Location** | Nairobi (Eastlands) |
| **Education** | University student (3rd year, UoN) |
| **Income** | KES 5,000/month (allowance + part-time work) |
| **Device** | Android smartphone (Infinix Hot 40i) |
| **Internet** | Mobile data (Safaricom) |

### Background
Sarah experiences anxiety and depression but has never told anyone. She grew up in a household where "feeling sad" was dismissed as laziness. Her university has a counseling center, but she's terrified someone might see her walking in. She can't afford private therapy and doesn't know any therapists.

### Goals
- Find someone safe to talk to about her anxiety
- Track her moods and understand her patterns
- Learn coping strategies without anyone knowing

### Frustrations
- "I can't afford therapy — even the university ones might tell my parents"
- "If my friends found out I'm depressed, they'd think I'm weak"
- "I don't even know where to start getting help"
- "Foreign apps don't understand Kenyan realities"

### How SafeHaven Helps
- ✅ Registers with a pseudonym — no one knows it's her
- ✅ Free — no payment required
- ✅ Encrypted journal tracks her moods over time
- ✅ Peer support forum lets her share without judgment
- ✅ Connects with trained volunteers who understand Kenyan context
- ✅ Quick exit button if someone looks at her phone

### Preferred Features
1. Encrypted Journal (daily use)
2. Peer Support Forum (weekly)
3. Volunteer Chat (when in crisis)
4. Quick Exit (always on)

## Persona 2: "Volunteer Victor" — The Trained Peer Listener

### Demographics
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Location** | Kisumu |
| **Education** | BSc Psychology (Maseno University) |
| **Occupation** | Community health worker + freelance counselor |
| **Income** | KES 25,000/month |
| **Device** | Android (Samsung A14) |

### Background
Victor studied psychology and has volunteered with local NGOs providing community mental health outreach. He's passionate about making mental health support accessible in western Kenya. He has training in crisis intervention and motivational interviewing, but lacks a formal clinical license.

### Goals
- Reach more people than his physical community allows
- Build his counseling portfolio and impact metrics
- Eventually transition to licensed professional status
- Feel that his work makes a tangible difference

### Frustrations
- "I can only reach people in my village during outreach days"
- "There's no platform where I can volunteer my skills digitally"
- "I want to help, but I need to know the people I'm helping are real and safe"
- "I need documentation of my volunteer hours for my license application"

### How SafeHaven Helps
- ✅ Volunteer dashboard shows impact metrics (views, chats)
- ✅ Digital platform lets him reach seekers across Kenya
- ✅ Peer listener track doesn't require a clinical license
- ✅ Admin-verified profile builds credibility
- ✅ Structured application process ensures quality

### Preferred Features
1. Volunteer Dashboard (daily)
2. Secure Chat with seekers (3-5 sessions/week)
3. Impact metrics (monthly review)

## Persona 4: "Corporate Chris" — The HR Director

### Demographics
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | 45 |
| **Location** | Nairobi (Upper Hill) |
| **Education** | MBA (Strathmore) |
| **Occupation** | Head of HR at a tech company (200 employees) |
| **Device** | Laptop + iPhone |

### Background
Chris manages HR for a growing Kenyan tech company. Post-COVID, he's seen a significant increase in employee burnout, absenteeism, and turnover. The company doesn't have an Employee Assistance Program (EAP) because most providers charge $50+/employee/month. He's looking for an affordable, anonymous solution.

### Goals
- Provide mental health support to employees without being intrusive
- Reduce absenteeism and turnover rates
- Stay within his wellness budget (KES 1M/year)
- Demonstrate ROI on wellness spending to the CEO

### Frustrations
- "International EAP providers are too expensive for a Kenyan company"
- "Employees won't use a program if they think HR can see their data"
- "I need usage reports without individual-level data"
- "Current wellness programs feel generic and unused"

### How SafeHaven Helps
- ✅ B2B Corporate Wellness plan at KES 500/employee/month
- ✅ Employees get anonymous access (HR cannot see individual data)
- ✅ Aggregate usage reports for ROI tracking
- ✅ Kenya-specific content and local volunteer network
- ✅ Within budget: 200 employees × KES 500 × 12 = KES 1.2M/year

### Preferred Features
1. Admin reporting dashboard (aggregate, no PII)
2. Employee onboarding materials
3. Usage analytics (monthly)

## Persona 5: "GBV Grace" — The Domestic Violence Survivor

### Demographics
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age** | 35 |
| **Location** | Nakuru (rural area) |
| **Education** | Secondary school |
| **Occupation** | Small-scale farmer |
| **Income** | KES 8,000/month |
| **Device** | Tecno feature phone (shared with family) |

### Background
Grace is in an abusive relationship. She cannot leave due to financial dependence and fear for her children. Her abuser monitors her phone. She's never told anyone about the abuse. The nearest police station is 15km away, and the gender desk officer is a friend of her husband.

### Goals
- Find someone to talk to who won't judge or expose her
- Create an escape plan without her abuser knowing
- Access legal information about her rights
- Find local safe houses or support organizations

### Frustrations
- "I can't save a help app on my phone — he checks everything"
- "If I call a hotline, someone might hear me"
- "I don't trust the local police"
- "I'm too ashamed to tell my family"

### How SafeHaven Helps
- ✅ Web-based (no app installation — harder to detect)
- ✅ Quick Exit button (instantly redirects to Google)
- ✅ No registration trace (pseudonymous, no email)
- ✅ Safety plan for escape planning
- ✅ Directory of GBV organizations and safe houses
- ✅ Anonymous forum for peer support from other survivors

### Critical Requirements
1. Quick Exit (essential — life-safety feature)
2. No app installation
3. Safety Plan (offline accessible)
4. GBV resource directory
5. Forum (anonymous survivor community)
