## Revenue Model Detail

### Primary Revenue (Year 1)

| Stream | Model | Target |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **B2B Corporate Wellness** | Monthly subscription per employee | KES 500/employee/month |
| **NGO Partnerships** | Platform licensing + data insights | KES 200K-500K/contract |
| **Grants** | WHO, USAID, Gates Foundation applications | KES 5M-15M |

### Secondary Revenue (Year 2+)

| Stream | Model | Target |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Government Contracts** | County health integration | KES 1M-3M/county |
| **Premium Features** | Group therapy scheduling, professional matching | KES 500/month |
| **Training Programs** | Peer listener certification courses | KES 5,000/course |

### What's Always Free
- ✅ Anonymous account creation
- ✅ Encrypted journal & safety plan
- ✅ Peer support forum access
- ✅ Community groups & events
- ✅ Resource library
- ✅ 1-on-1 chat with peer listeners
- ✅ Crisis resources & hotlines

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cost to Acquire User (CAU) | KES 150 (~$1.15) |

| B2B LTV : CAC ratio | 8:1 |
| Breakeven point | Month 18 |

## Key Assumptions

1. **Kenyan youth are ready for digital mental health** — Validated by M-Pesa adoption patterns and COVID-era telemedicine growth.
2. **Anonymity drives adoption** — Supported by research showing 60% avoid therapy due to stigma.
3. **Volunteer supply is sustainable** — Kenya produces 2,000+ psychology graduates annually; peer listener model further expands supply.
4. **B2B is the primary monetization path** — Corporate wellness is a $4.5B global market; Kenyan corporates are beginning to invest.
5. **Grants bridge the gap to profitability** — Mental health in Africa is a priority area for WHO, USAID, and Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.
